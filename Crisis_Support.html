üõü What to Do Right Now

  • Call 911 if you're in immediate danger
  • Go to the nearest emergency room if you can safely get there
  • Tell someone you trust how you're feeling - a friend, family member, or counselor
  • Put away anything harmful and ask someone to stay with you
  • Avoid alcohol or substances that might make things worse

üí¨ When You Call a Crisis Line

Step 1: Say Your Situation

"I'm having thoughts of hurting myself and I need help" or "I'm in a very unsafe place right now"

Step 2: Be Honest About Severity

"I have a plan and access to means" - Crisis counselors need this info to best help you

Step 3: Tell Them Your Location

Let them know where you are so they can direct emergency services if needed

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I should reach out to a professional?

If you're struggling with symptoms for more than 2 weeks, they're interfering with daily life, or self-help strategies aren't working, it's time to reach out. Mental health professionals can offer support that goes beyond what online tools can provide.

Is it okay to use these tools instead of therapy?

These tools are complements to, not replacements for, professional care. They're great for ongoing self-care, but therapy offers personalized support that tools can't provide. Use both together for best results.

What should I say when calling a crisis line?

Be honest about how you're feeling. You can say: "I'm having thoughts of harming myself," or "I'm having a panic attack and I don't know what to do." Crisis counselors are trained to help, and nothing you say will shock them.

How do I help someone else in crisis?

Listen without judgment. Ask directly: "Are you thinking about harming yourself?" If they say yes, help them contact emergency services or a crisis line. Stay with them. Remove access to harmful means. Tell them they matter and you care about them.
